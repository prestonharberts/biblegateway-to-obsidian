# 1 Kings 4
##### <span class=arrow-left></span>[[1kings3|Prev]]<span class=navigation-separator></span>[[1kings4notes|Notes]]<span class=navigation-separator></span>[[1kings5|Next]]<span class=arrow-right></span>
## Solomon’s Royal Court and Administrators
###### 1
<span class=verse-first>1</span>King Solomon ruled over all Israel.
###### 2
<span class=verse-body>2</span>These were his officials:
<div class=paragraph-break></div>

Azariah son of Zadok was the priest.
###### 3
<span class=verse-body>3</span>Elihoreph and Ahijah, the sons of Shisha, wrote down what happened.
<div class=paragraph-break></div>

Jehoshaphat son of Ahilud was in charge of the records.
###### 4
<span class=verse-body>4</span>Benaiah son of Jehoiada was commander of the army.
<div class=paragraph-break></div>

Zadok and Abiathar were priests.
###### 5
<span class=verse-body>5</span>Azariah son of Nathan was supervisor of the district governors.
<div class=paragraph-break></div>

Zabud son of Nathan was a priest and adviser to the king.
###### 6
<span class=verse-body>6</span>Ahishar was supervisor of the palace.
<div class=paragraph-break></div>

Adoniram son of Abda was supervisor of the work crews.
###### 7
<span class=verse-body>7</span>Solomon had twelve district governors appointed throughout Israel who acquired supplies for the king and his palace. Each was responsible for one month in the year.
###### 8
<span class=verse-body>8</span>These were their names:
<div class=paragraph-break></div>

Ben Hur was in charge of the hill country of Ephraim.
###### 9
<span class=verse-body>9</span>Ben Deker was in charge of Makaz, Shaalbim, Beth Shemesh, and Elon Beth Hanan.
###### 10
<span class=verse-body>10</span>Ben Hesed was in charge of Arubboth; he controlled Socoh and all the territory of Hepher.
###### 11
<span class=verse-body>11</span>Ben Abinadab was in charge of Naphath Dor. (He was married to Solomon’s daughter Taphath.)
###### 12
<span class=verse-body>12</span>Baana son of Ahilud was in charge of Taanach and Megiddo, as well as all of Beth Shean next to Zarethan below Jezreel, from Beth Shean to Abel Meholah and on past Jokmeam.
###### 13
<span class=verse-body>13</span>Ben Geber was in charge of Ramoth Gilead; he controlled the villages of Jair son of Manasseh in Gilead, as well as the region of Argob in Bashan, including sixty large walled cities with bronze bars locking their gates.
###### 14
<span class=verse-body>14</span>Ahinadab son of Iddo was in charge of Mahanaim.
###### 15
<span class=verse-body>15</span>Ahimaaz was in charge of Naphtali. (He married Solomon’s daughter Basemath.)
###### 16
<span class=verse-body>16</span>Baana son of Hushai was in charge of Asher and Aloth.
###### 17
<span class=verse-body>17</span>Jehoshaphat son of Paruah was in charge of Issachar.
###### 18
<span class=verse-body>18</span>Shimei son of Ela was in charge of Benjamin.
###### 19
<span class=verse-body>19</span>Geber son of Uri was in charge of the land of Gilead (the territory which had once belonged to King Sihon of the Amorites and to King Og of Bashan). He was sole governor of the area.
## Solomon’s Wealth and Fame
###### 20
<span class=verse-first>20</span>The people of Judah and Israel were as innumerable as the sand on the seashore; they had plenty to eat and drink and were happy.
<div class=paragraph-break></div>

###### 21
<span class=verse-first>21</span> Solomon ruled all the kingdoms from the Euphrates River to the land of the Philistines, as far as the border of Egypt. These kingdoms paid tribute as Solomon’s subjects throughout his lifetime.
###### 22
<span class=verse-first>22</span>Each day Solomon’s royal court consumed thirty cors of finely milled flour, sixty cors of cereal,
###### 23
<span class=verse-body>23</span>ten calves fattened in the stall, 20 calves from the pasture, and 100 sheep, not to mention rams, gazelles, deer, and well-fed birds.
###### 24
<span class=verse-body>24</span>His royal court was so large because he ruled over all the kingdoms west of the Euphrates River from Tiphsah to Gaza; he was at peace with all his neighbors.
###### 25
<span class=verse-body>25</span>All the people of Judah and Israel had security; everyone from Dan to Beer Sheba enjoyed the produce of their vines and fig trees throughout Solomon’s lifetime.
###### 26
<span class=verse-body>26</span>Solomon had 4,000 stalls for his chariot horses and 12,000 horses.
###### 27
<span class=verse-body>27</span>The district governors acquired supplies for King Solomon and all who ate in his royal palace. Each was responsible for one month in the year; they made sure nothing was lacking.
###### 28
<span class=verse-body>28</span>Each one also brought to the assigned location his quota of barley and straw for the various horses.
<div class=paragraph-break></div>

###### 29
<span class=verse-first>29</span>God gave Solomon wisdom and very great discernment; the breadth of his understanding was as infinite as the sand on the seashore.
###### 30
<span class=verse-body>30</span>Solomon was wiser than all the men of the east and all the sages of Egypt.
###### 31
<span class=verse-body>31</span>He was wiser than any man, including Ethan the Ezrahite or Heman, Calcol, and Darda, the sons of Mahol. He was famous in all the neighboring nations.
###### 32
<span class=verse-body>32</span>He composed 3,000 proverbs and 1,005 songs.
###### 33
<span class=verse-body>33</span>He produced manuals on botany, describing every kind of plant, from the cedars of Lebanon to the hyssop that grows on walls. He also produced manuals on biology, describing animals, birds, insects, and fish.
###### 34
<span class=verse-body>34</span>People from all nations came to hear Solomon’s display of wisdom; they came from all the kings of the earth who heard about his wisdom.

# Psalm 105
##### <span class=arrow-left></span>[[ps104|Prev]]<span class=navigation-separator></span>[[ps105notes|Notes]]<span class=navigation-separator></span>[[ps106|Next]]<span class=arrow-right></span>
###### 1
><span class=verse-first-poetry>1</span>Give thanks to the Lᴏʀᴅ.
>Call on his name.
>Make known his accomplishments among the nations.
###### 2
><span class=verse-body-poetry>2</span>Sing to him.
>Make music to him.
>Tell about all his miraculous deeds.
###### 3
><span class=verse-body-poetry>3</span>Boast about his holy name.
>Let the hearts of those who seek the Lᴏʀᴅ rejoice.
###### 4
><span class=verse-body-poetry>4</span>Seek the Lᴏʀᴅ and the strength he gives.
>Seek his presence continually.
###### 5
><span class=verse-body-poetry>5</span>Recall the miraculous deeds he performed,
>His mighty acts and the judgments he decreed,
###### 6
><span class=verse-body-poetry>6</span>O children of Abraham, God’s servant,
>You descendants of Jacob, God’s chosen ones.
###### 7
><span class=verse-body-poetry>7</span>He is the Lᴏʀᴅ our God;
>He carries out judgment throughout the earth.
<div class=paragraph-break></div>

###### 8
><span class=verse-first-poetry>8</span>He always remembers his covenantal decree,
>The promise he made to a thousand generations—
###### 9
><span class=verse-body-poetry>9</span>The promise he made to Abraham,
>The promise he made by oath to Isaac.
###### 10
><span class=verse-body-poetry>10</span>He gave it to Jacob as a decree,
>To Israel as a lasting promise,
###### 11
><span class=verse-body-poetry>11</span>Saying, “To you I will give the land of Canaan
><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>As the portion of your inheritance.”
###### 12
><span class=verse-body-poetry>12</span>When they were few in number,
>Just a very few, and resident foreigners within it,
###### 13
><span class=verse-body-poetry>13</span>They wandered from nation to nation,
>And from one kingdom to another.
###### 14
><span class=verse-body-poetry>14</span>He let no one oppress them;
>He disciplined kings for their sake,
###### 15
><span class=verse-body-poetry>15</span>Saying, “Don’t touch my chosen ones.
><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>Don’t harm my prophets.”
<div class=paragraph-break></div>

###### 16
><span class=verse-first-poetry>16</span>He called down a famine upon the earth;
>He cut off all the food supply.
###### 17
><span class=verse-body-poetry>17</span>He sent a man ahead of them—
>Joseph was sold as a servant.
###### 18
><span class=verse-body-poetry>18</span>The shackles hurt his feet;
>His neck was placed in an iron collar,
###### 19
><span class=verse-body-poetry>19</span>Until the time when his prediction came true.
>The Lᴏʀᴅ’s word proved him right.
###### 20
><span class=verse-body-poetry>20</span>The king authorized his release;
>The ruler of nations set him free.
###### 21
><span class=verse-body-poetry>21</span>He put him in charge of his palace,
>And made him manager of all his property,
###### 22
><span class=verse-body-poetry>22</span>Giving him authority to imprison his officials
>And to teach his advisers.
###### 23
><span class=verse-body-poetry>23</span>Israel moved to Egypt;
>Jacob lived for a time in the land of Ham.
###### 24
><span class=verse-body-poetry>24</span>The Lᴏʀᴅ made his people very fruitful,
>And made them more numerous than their enemies.
<div class=paragraph-break></div>

###### 25
><span class=verse-first-poetry>25</span>He caused the Egyptians to hate his people,
>And to mistreat his servants.
###### 26
><span class=verse-body-poetry>26</span>He sent his servant Moses,
>And Aaron, whom he had chosen.
###### 27
><span class=verse-body-poetry>27</span>They executed his miraculous signs among them,
>And his amazing deeds in the land of Ham.
###### 28
><span class=verse-body-poetry>28</span>He made it dark;
>Moses and Aaron did not disobey his orders.
###### 29
><span class=verse-body-poetry>29</span>He turned the Egyptians’ water into blood,
>And killed their fish.
###### 30
><span class=verse-body-poetry>30</span>Their land was overrun by frogs,
>Which even got into the rooms of their kings.
###### 31
><span class=verse-body-poetry>31</span>He ordered flies to come;
>Gnats invaded their whole territory.
###### 32
><span class=verse-body-poetry>32</span>He sent hail along with the rain;
>There was lightning in their land.
###### 33
><span class=verse-body-poetry>33</span>He destroyed their vines and fig trees,
>And broke the trees throughout their territory.
###### 34
><span class=verse-body-poetry>34</span>He ordered locusts to come,
>Innumerable grasshoppers.
###### 35
><span class=verse-body-poetry>35</span>They ate all the vegetation in their land,
>And devoured the crops of their fields.
###### 36
><span class=verse-body-poetry>36</span>He struck down all the firstborn in their land,
>The firstfruits of their reproductive power.
<div class=paragraph-break></div>

###### 37
><span class=verse-first-poetry>37</span>He brought his people out enriched with silver and gold;
>None of his tribes stumbled.
###### 38
><span class=verse-body-poetry>38</span>Egypt was happy when they left,
>For they were afraid of them.
###### 39
><span class=verse-body-poetry>39</span>He spread out a cloud for a cover,
>And provided a fire to light up the night.
###### 40
><span class=verse-body-poetry>40</span>They asked for food, and he sent quail;
>He satisfied them with food from the sky.
###### 41
><span class=verse-body-poetry>41</span>He opened up a rock and water flowed out;
>A river ran through dry regions.
###### 42
><span class=verse-body-poetry>42</span>Yes, he remembered the sacred promise
>He made to Abraham his servant.
###### 43
><span class=verse-body-poetry>43</span>When he led his people out, they rejoiced;
>His chosen ones shouted with joy.
###### 44
><span class=verse-body-poetry>44</span>He handed the territory of nations over to them,
>And they took possession of what other peoples had produced,
###### 45
><span class=verse-body-poetry>45</span>So that they might keep his commands
>And obey his laws.
>Praise the Lᴏʀᴅ.

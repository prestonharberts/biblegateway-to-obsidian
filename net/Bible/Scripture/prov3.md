# Proverbs 3

## Exhortations to Seek Wisdom and Walk with the Lord
###### 1
><span class=verse-first-poetry>1</span>My child, do not forget my teaching,
>But let your heart keep my commandments,
###### 2
><span class=verse-body-poetry>2</span>For they will provide a long and full life,
>And well-being for you.
###### 3
><span class=verse-body-poetry>3</span>Do not let mercy and truth leave you;
>Bind them around your neck,
>Write them on the tablet of your heart.
###### 4
><span class=verse-body-poetry>4</span>Then you will find favor and good understanding,
>In the sight of God and people.
###### 5
><span class=verse-body-poetry>5</span>Trust in the Lᴏʀᴅ with all your heart,
>And do not rely on your own understanding.
###### 6
><span class=verse-body-poetry>6</span>Acknowledge him in all your ways,
>And he will make your paths straight.
###### 7
><span class=verse-body-poetry>7</span>Do not be wise in your own estimation;
>Fear the Lᴏʀᴅ and turn away from evil.
###### 8
><span class=verse-body-poetry>8</span>This will bring healing to your body,
>And refreshment to your inner self.
###### 9
><span class=verse-body-poetry>9</span>Honor the Lᴏʀᴅ from your wealth
>And from the firstfruits of all your crops;
###### 10
><span class=verse-body-poetry>10</span>Then your barns will be filled completely,
>And your vats will overflow with new wine.
###### 11
><span class=verse-body-poetry>11</span>My child, do not despise discipline from the Lᴏʀᴅ,
>And do not loathe his rebuke.
###### 12
><span class=verse-body-poetry>12</span>For the Lᴏʀᴅ disciplines those he loves,
>Just as a father disciplines the son in whom he delights.
## Blessings of Obtaining Wisdom
###### 13
><span class=verse-first-poetry>13</span>Blessed is the one who has found wisdom,
>And the one who obtains understanding.
###### 14
><span class=verse-body-poetry>14</span>For her benefit is more profitable than silver,
>And her gain is better than gold.
###### 15
><span class=verse-body-poetry>15</span>She is more precious than rubies,
>And none of the things you desire can compare with her.
###### 16
><span class=verse-body-poetry>16</span>Long life is in her right hand;
>In her left hand are riches and honor.
###### 17
><span class=verse-body-poetry>17</span>Her ways are very pleasant,
>And all her paths are peaceful.
###### 18
><span class=verse-body-poetry>18</span>She is like a tree of life to those who grasp onto her,
>And everyone who takes hold of her will be blessed.
###### 19
><span class=verse-body-poetry>19</span>By wisdom the Lᴏʀᴅ laid the foundation of the earth;
>He established the heavens by understanding.
###### 20
><span class=verse-body-poetry>20</span>By his knowledge the primordial sea was broken open,
>So that the clouds drip down dew.
###### 21
><span class=verse-body-poetry>21</span>My child, do not let them escape from your sight;
>Safeguard sound wisdom and discretion.
###### 22
><span class=verse-body-poetry>22</span>So they will become life for your soul,
>And grace around your neck.
###### 23
><span class=verse-body-poetry>23</span>Then you will walk on your way with security,
>And you will not stumble.
###### 24
><span class=verse-body-poetry>24</span>When you lie down you will not be filled with fear;
>When you lie down your sleep will be pleasant.
###### 25
><span class=verse-body-poetry>25</span>Do not be afraid of sudden disaster,
>Or when destruction overtakes the wicked;
###### 26
><span class=verse-body-poetry>26</span>For the Lᴏʀᴅ will be the source of your confidence,
>And he will guard your foot from being caught in a trap.
## Wisdom Demonstrated in Relationships with People
###### 27
><span class=verse-first-poetry>27</span>Do not withhold good from those who need it,
>When you have the ability to help.
###### 28
><span class=verse-body-poetry>28</span>Do not say to your neighbor, “Go! Return tomorrow
><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>And I will give it,” when you have it with you at the time.
###### 29
><span class=verse-body-poetry>29</span>Do not plot evil against your neighbor
>When he dwells by you unsuspectingly.
###### 30
><span class=verse-body-poetry>30</span>Do not accuse anyone without legitimate cause,
>If he has not treated you wrongly.
###### 31
><span class=verse-body-poetry>31</span>Do not envy a violent man,
>And do not choose any of his ways;
###### 32
><span class=verse-body-poetry>32</span>For one who goes astray is an abomination to the Lᴏʀᴅ,
>But he reveals his intimate counsel to the upright.
###### 33
><span class=verse-body-poetry>33</span>The Lᴏʀᴅ’s curse is on the household of the wicked,
>But he blesses the home of the righteous.
###### 34
><span class=verse-body-poetry>34</span>With arrogant scoffers he is scornful,
>Yet he shows favor to the humble.
###### 35
><span class=verse-body-poetry>35</span>The wise inherit honor,
>But he holds fools up to public contempt.

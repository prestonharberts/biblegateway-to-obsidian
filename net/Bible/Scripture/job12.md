# Job 12

## Job’s Reply to Zophar
###### 1
<span class=verse-first>1</span>Then Job answered:
<div class=paragraph-break></div>

###### 2
><span class=verse-body-poetry>2</span><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>Without a doubt you are the people,
><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>And wisdom will die with you.
###### 3
><span class=verse-body-poetry>3</span><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>I also have understanding as well as you;
><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>I am not inferior to you.
><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>Who does not know such things as these?
###### 4
><span class=verse-body-poetry>4</span><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>I am a laughingstock to my friends,
><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>I, who called on God and whom he answered—
><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>A righteous and blameless man
><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>Is a laughingstock!
###### 5
><span class=verse-body-poetry>5</span><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>For calamity, there is derision
><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>(according to the ideas of the fortunate)—
><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>A fate for those whose feet slip.
###### 6
><span class=verse-body-poetry>6</span><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>But the tents of robbers are peaceful,
><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>And those who provoke God are confident—
><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>Who carry their god in their hands.”
## Knowledge of God’s Wisdom
###### 7
><span class=verse-first-poetry>7</span><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>But now, ask the animals and they will teach you,
><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>Or the birds of the sky and they will tell you.
###### 8
><span class=verse-body-poetry>8</span><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>Or speak to the earth and it will teach you,
><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>Or let the fish of the sea declare to you.
###### 9
><span class=verse-body-poetry>9</span><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>Which of all these does not know
><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>That the hand of the Lᴏʀᴅ has done this?
###### 10
><span class=verse-body-poetry>10</span><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>In his hand is the life of every creature
><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>And the breath of all the human race.
###### 11
><span class=verse-body-poetry>11</span><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>Does not the ear test words,
><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>As the tongue tastes food?
###### 12
><span class=verse-body-poetry>12</span><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>Is not wisdom found among the aged?
><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>Does not long life bring understanding?
<div class=paragraph-break></div>

###### 13
><span class=verse-first-poetry>13</span><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>With God are wisdom and power;
><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>Counsel and understanding are his.
###### 14
><span class=verse-body-poetry>14</span><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>If he tears down, it cannot be rebuilt;
><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>If he imprisons a person, there is no escape.
###### 15
><span class=verse-body-poetry>15</span><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>If he holds back the waters, then they dry up;
><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>If he releases them, they destroy the land.
###### 16
><span class=verse-body-poetry>16</span><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>With him are strength and prudence;
><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>Both the one who goes astray
><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>And the one who misleads are his.
###### 17
><span class=verse-body-poetry>17</span><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>He leads counselors away stripped
><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>And makes judges into fools.
###### 18
><span class=verse-body-poetry>18</span><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>He loosens the bonds of kings
><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>And binds a loincloth around their waist.
###### 19
><span class=verse-body-poetry>19</span><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>He leads priests away stripped
><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>And overthrows the potentates.
###### 20
><span class=verse-body-poetry>20</span><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>He deprives the trusted advisers of speech
><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>And takes away the discernment of elders.
###### 21
><span class=verse-body-poetry>21</span><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>He pours contempt on noblemen
><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>And disarms the powerful.
###### 22
><span class=verse-body-poetry>22</span><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>He reveals the deep things of darkness,
><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>And brings deep shadows into the light.
###### 23
><span class=verse-body-poetry>23</span><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>He makes nations great, and destroys them;
><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>He extends the boundaries of nations
><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>And disperses them.
###### 24
><span class=verse-body-poetry>24</span><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>He deprives the leaders of the earth
><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>Of their understanding;
><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>He makes them wander
><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>In a trackless desert waste.
###### 25
><span class=verse-body-poetry>25</span><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>They grope about in darkness without light;
><span class=poetry-quote-double>“</span>He makes them stagger like drunkards.
